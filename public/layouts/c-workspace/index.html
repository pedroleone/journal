Wednesday, March 19

Today felt like a turning point. The conversation with Maria about the project direction was exactly what I needed to hear. She has this way of cutting through the noise and getting to what actually matters.

I think the key insight was that we've been optimizing for the wrong metric entirely. Not revenue per user, but time to first meaningful action. That reframing changes everything about how we should think about onboarding.

We spent an hour going through the user research and I kept finding myself nodding — the signals were there all along, we just weren't asking the right questions. Maria pointed out three specific moments in the onboarding flow where people hesitate, and in each case the hesitation isn't about capability, it's about confidence. They don't know if they're doing it right.

So the redesign isn't really about the surface. It's about trust signals. Small things: confirmation of progress, clear reversibility, showing that nothing is permanent until it needs to be. That reframing changes everything about how we should think about onboarding.
