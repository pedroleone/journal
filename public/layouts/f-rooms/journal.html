Today felt like a turning point. The conversation with Maria about the project direction was exactly what I needed to hear. She has this way of cutting through the noise and getting to what actually matters. I keep thinking about what she said about intention — that most of what we call planning is just anxiety wearing a calendar. I left that meeting feeling lighter than I have in weeks. Something shifted. Not in the project itself, but in how I'm holding it. Spent the afternoon at the usual corner table. The light was good. Wrote for almost two hours without looking up, which hasn't happened in a long time. The words came out rough but they came out. That's the part that matters right now. There's a kind of stubbornness to good writing — you have to sit with the blank space long enough that it stops feeling threatening. Today I sat. Today it worked.
